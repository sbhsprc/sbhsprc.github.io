About Us

The Computer Programming and Robotics Club at Stone Bridge High School is a club dedicatd to learning about computers, how to use them, and how to build them. We participate in a wide variety of activities including the Air Force's Cyber Security program CyberPatriot, Vex Robotics, and creating video games.

The Club is run by President Shubham Patil, with help from Secretary and Webmaster Jeremy Stalcup. The Programming division of the club is lead by Jordan Hale, the Robotics division is run by Nate Wooley, while the Cyber Security division is lead by Alex Johnson.

Cyber Security

The cyber security division of the Stone Bridge Programming and Robotics Club competes in various cyber security competitions throughout the year. The main competition for the cyber security division is Cyber Patriot, which is a cyber security competition designed to educate participants on defensive security skills. The cyber security division also competes in large games of capture the flag, which give competitors a chance to demonstrate their offensive and defensive cyber security skills in addition to analytical thinking. The cyber security team has been very successful in the time that it has existed, and has won scholarships, accolades, and national recognition. As a division, we look forward to whatever cyber security challenges may greet us in the future.

Robotics

The robotics portion of our club is primarily about VEX robotics. Each year VEX releases a new challenge, and robots are built and programmed to compete in the challenge. The challenge for 2015-2016 is VEX Skyrise, in which a robot must stack cubes on poles of varying height, and stack Skyrise segments. While not at competitions, we focus on building and programming the best robot we can, for the competition season. This is filled with design, building, programming and driving phases, all in preparation for the competitions. We have revieved many top 10 placements at quite of few local VEX competitions.
